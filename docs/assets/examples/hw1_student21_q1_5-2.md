*constant current of 10A entering the positive voltage terminal with a voltage of 110V.
*ran for 2 hrs

a) Find the charge in coulombs that passes through the range
$i = \frac{dq}{dt}$
2 hrs $\xrightarrow{60}$ 120 min $\xrightarrow{60}$ 7200 sec
$$ q = \int_{-\infty}^{t} i d\tau = \int_{0}^{7200} 10 d\tau + q(0) $$
$$ = 10\tau + q(0) \bigg|_0^{7200} $$
$$ = [10(7200s) + q(0)] - [10(0) + q(0)] $$
$$ 72000 + q(0) - 0 - q(0) $$
$$ q = 72000 C $$

b) Find the power absorbed by the range
> A simple circuit diagram shows a single component, a box labeled "110V". The voltage polarity is marked with a '+' on the left and a '-' on the right. A current arrow labeled 'i' enters the positive terminal on the left.

$$ p = v \cdot i $$
$$ p = (110V) \cdot (10A) $$
$$ = 1100 W $$

c) If electric energy costs 12 cents per kilowatt-hour, determine the cost of operating the range for 2 hours
$$ \text{cost} = p \cdot .12\$ $$
$$ = (1.1 kW \cdot .12\$) \cdot 2 $$
$$ = 26.4 \text{¢} \rightarrow \$0.264 $$
